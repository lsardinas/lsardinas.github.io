Values and principles

We integrate Christian beliefs and principles in some therapeutic processes. In these approaches, we draw on Christian teachings, values, and spirituality to guide the therapeutic work with clients with Christian faith.

Values

  • Empathy and Compassion

    A commitment to understanding and sharing the feelings of clients, fostering a compassionate and non-judgmental therapeutic relationship.

  • Ethical Practice

    Adherence to a strong ethical framework, including confidentiality, informed consent, and a commitment to the well-being and rights of clients.

  • Cultural sensitivity

    Recognition and respect for the diverse backgrounds, cultures, and identities of clients, with a commitment to providing culturally sensitive and inclusive care.

  • Client-Centered Approach

    Prioritizing the client's needs, goals, and autonomy in the therapeutic process, fostering collaboration and shared decision-making.

  • Transparency

    Open and honest communication with clients about the therapeutic process, expectations, and any relevant information, promoting trust and collaboration.

Principles

We may use biblical principles and teachings as a foundation for addressing various mental health issues. This could include exploring issues such as forgiveness, grace, love, and the importance of community.

  • Prayer and Spirituality

    Prayer and spirituality are often integral components of Christian faith psychotherapy. We may encourage clients to incorporate prayer into their healing process and explore how their faith can provide strength and guidance.

  • Value of Community and Support

    We emphasize the importance of community and support within the context of a church or Christian fellowship. Building a sense of belonging and connection with others is often seen as a crucial aspect of mental health.

  • Holistic Approach

    We take a holistic approach, addressing not only mental and emotional well-being but also considering the spiritual, physical, and social aspects of a person's life.

  • Respect for Diversity of Beliefs

    We respect the diversity of beliefs among clients. While Christian principles guide the therapy, therapists are generally trained to be sensitive to clients with different religious or spiritual backgrounds
